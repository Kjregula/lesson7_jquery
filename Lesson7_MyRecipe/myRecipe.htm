Blooming Onion

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup of mayonnaise
  • 3/4 tablespoon of ketchup
  • 2 tablespoons of horseradish sauce
  • 1/4 teaspoon of paprika
  • 1/4 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 pinch of ground black pepper
  • 1 pinch of cayenne pepper
  • 1 egg
  • 1 cup of milk
  • 1 cup of flour
  • 1 & 1/2 teaspoons of salt
  • 1 & 1/2 teaspoons of cayenne pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon of ground black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon of garlic powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • 1/4 teaspoon of dried oregano
  • 1/8 teaspoon of ground cumin
  • 1 large onion
  • 2 quarts of vegetable oil for deep-frying

Instructions

Preheat the oil to 350 degrees Fahrenheit.

Dipping sauce: Combine the mayonnaise, ketchup, horseradish sauce, paprika, 1/4 teaspoon of salt, 1 dash of black pepper, and 1 dash of cayenne pepper into a small bowl and refrigerate until needed.

Beat egg and combine with milk in a large bowl that can hold an onion. In another bowl combine the flour, salt, cayenne pepper, black pepper, garlic powder, thyme, oregano, and cumin.

Cut the onion around 3/4 of an inch off the top and bottom and remove the skin. Remove the 1-inch core and slice down the center of the onion about 3/4 of the way down. Repeat for every 90 degrees and then half of each section, until you have 16 sections. To make coating easier, spread the petals apart.

Dip the onion in the milk and egg mixture and then coat it in the flour mixture. Be sure to seperate the petals and sprinkle the flour mixture inbetween each petal. Dip the onion into the milk and egg mixture again and repeat the flour mixture process.

Place the onion in the refrigerator for 15 minutes.

Carefully place the onion into the oil and make sure the onion is fully submerged.

Fry the onion for 10 minutes or until golden brown. Remove the onion from the oil and drain on paper towels.

Open the center of the onion wide enough as to fit a small bowl. Put the dipping sauce into a small bowl and place in the center.

Enjoy.
